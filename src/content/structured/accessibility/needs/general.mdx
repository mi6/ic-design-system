## Designing for accessibility

Designing a page before development allows people to test features before a final design is selected. Creating multiple designs will give a good overview of the features that people want.

People can interact with the accessible designs and provide feedback on what does and doesn’t work.

This page displays the generic design implementations that can be included that benefit multiple forms of accessibility. The following suggestions are the required basics when designing a page or service.

### Large font

Writing in a large font size will put less writing on a page and make the words more readable.

Our eyes are most often drawn to the larger text on a page. Less words on a page will feel like less effort to read.

### Tabular formatted buttons

Allowing people to tab between each button instead of using the mouse.

Everything must be be accessible with this method for people who don’t like using the mouse to click into sections.

### Descriptive buttons

Ensure that buttons describe their feature. Having a button or link that says ‘Click here’ isn’t beneficial if ‘here’ isn’t specified.

Using descriptions stating exactly what the button will do is more accessible.

### Simple linear layouts

Ensure data is displayed in a linear format that is readable for people. Don’t use confusing layouts.
