Statement for [app name]

This accessibility statement applies to [app name] hosted at [url]. This app is run by [team name] as part of [name of organisation].

We want as many people as possible to be able to use this app. That means we’ve built the app so that you can:

  • zoom in up to 300% without the text spilling off the screen
  • navigate all of the app using just a keyboard
  • navigate all of the app using speech recognition software
  • use all of the app with a screen reader (including the most recent versions of JAWS and VoiceOver)
  • change most colours, contrast levels and fonts (using your browser)

We've also made the text in the app (including user guides) as simple as possible to understand.

AbilityNet has advice on making your device easier to use if you have different needs. Search your intranet or contact your IT Service Desk if you're using an offline system.

We know some parts of this app are not fully accessible:

  • the flowchart designer cannot be used with a screen reader
  • most older PDF documents are not fully accessible to screen reader software
  • the form builder has a dialog that cannot be closed using the keyboard when you click the 'extended settings' button

If you need information from this app in a different format like an accessible PDF or in an e-mail, contact your IT Service Desk.

If you need to create or modify a workflow in the app, contact your IT Service Desk for the temporary workaround. They will provide you with a Word document to complete and they will create or update the workflow for you.

If you need to modify 'extended settings' in the form builder, contact your IT Service Desk for the temporary workaround. They will ask you what settings you want to change, and the change will be done for you.

Enforcement Procedure

The Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) is responsible for enforcing the Public Sector Bodies (Websites and Mobile Applications) (No. 2) Accessibility Regulations 2018 (the 'accessibility regulations').

Due to the nature of the UKIC, breaches to the above accessibility regulations should be handled following internal procedures by the organisation that owns the app. These procedures must be transparent and accessible.

The responsible organisation for [app name] is [name of organisation].

If you are not satisfied with the accessibility of this app, consider contacting [team name] using the details below. You can also raise an issue with your local IT Service Desk or contact your HR representatives. Your Line Manager or local advocacy group(s) can also raise issues if you prefer to do so anonymously.

The app hosts documents that describe Policy, Process and Procedure for the organisations. The accessibility regulations apply to content hosted within the app even if it is authored from another tool.

Because the policy documents hosted on Portal are core to the Services it provides, these documents must be accessible. The regulations require that this applies to documents published before 23rd September 2018 because they are core to the Services of the app.

‘Guidance’ attachments created before 23rd September 2018 are therefore exempt and are not assured to be accessible at this time. We commit to flagging non-accessible attachments clearly in the app. We commit to requiring content contributors to improve the accessibility of their content, or risk it being removed.

For the purposes of this app, an ‘accessible’ document (PDF, PPTX, XLSX or DOCX) is one that is tested using built in assessment tools of the Adobe/Microsoft Word tools respectively. This includes correct use of headings, alt-text, icons and colour.

Interactive tools

Some of the interactive flowchart and form creation tools are difficult to navigate using a keyboard and screen reader. For example, because the workflow builder relies on visuals.

The flowchart builder and form builder are built using third party software and 'skinned' to look like our website.

We've assessed the [effort/time] cost of fixing the issues these tools. We believe that doing so at the current time would be a disproportionate burden within the meaning of the accessibility regulations. Currently we are piloting the app with a user group of less than 25 users. This will be re-assessed frequently as the app is rolled out and the user base grows.

This workflow/form functionality is not ‘core’ to the app. This functionality is limited to a group of users who need to – and have been trained on – building workflows and forms.

Before the app is published we commit to ensuring the issues are fixed or that there is an equitable alternative for users to requiring this functionality.

Content that's not within the scope of the accessibility regulations

[Note: in this subsection list accessibility problems that fall outside the scope of the accessibility regulations.]

PDFs and other documents

The accessibility regulations do not require us to fix PDFs or other documents published before 23 September 2018 if they are not essential to providing the Services of the app. Therefore we do not plan to fix non-Policy attachments published before 23 September 2018. The must be made accessible if the document is substantially reviewed.

Any new PDFs or Word documents, including substantial revisions to content produced before 23rd September 2018, will meet accessibility standards.
